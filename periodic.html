Periodic Table

What is the Periodic Table?

The periodic table of elements, pictured above, is they way in which elements are sorted and organized.

What does it consist of?

The table consists of seven periods, rows, and eighteen groups, columns, and is arranged in order of increasing atomic number.

The elements are also grouped by properties as denoted by the different colors: (going across the groups) Alkali Metals, Alkaline-Earth Metals, Transition Metals, Other Metals, Other Non-Metals, Halogens, Noble Gases, (the two rows at the bottom) Actinoid Elements and Lanthanoid Elements.

Elements in the same group have similar properties such as reactivity and typically they all have the same charge, too, when doing problems involving the element. A catchy way to learn the order and names of the element in the form of a song by ASAP Science.
